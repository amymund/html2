Oscar Wilde

The Picture of Dorian Gray

The Picture of Dorian Gray, Oscar Wilde's sole novel, focuses on a young man who wishes to remain forever young and beautiful by having his portrait to bear the burden of his aging and the weight of his sins. Set in late 19th-century Victorian England, the story follows Dorian Gray's descent into hedonism, spurred by his newfound disdain for the natural aging process and any form of restraint. What follows is a tale of narcissism and moral corruption with eerie supernatural twists.
